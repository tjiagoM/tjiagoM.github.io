Supervised Classification for Brain Graph Data

We can see the brain as graph, in which nodes are brain regions and edges either a physical or a functional connection. Thus, we can tailor machine learning and deep learning methodologies for graphs [1,2] to aid physicians. The aim of this project is to use multi-modal graphs of healthy people (big data available from the Human Connectome Project) to predict categorical or continuous variables (e.g. clinical disability score, personality score, â€¦). Understand and interpret what factors contribute to those predictions is extremely interesting for clinicians as an application, and can lead to new methodological developments.
